Where Australia's parrots are seen — a taxonomic tree

Fifteen species of Australian parrot, arranged by taxonomy from order Psittaciformes down through family, genus and species. Each species leaf (end node) connects to a small bar chart showing the share of that species' observation records across the eight states and territories, in a standardised order from left-to-right: WA, SA, VIC, TAS, ACT, NSW, QLD, NT. Node size in the tree is proportional to log of observation count, so abundant species like the Galah and Rainbow Lorikeet with around a million records each can be compared alongside the critically endangered Orange-bellied Parrot with approximately 500 observations. The bar-chart shapes make biogeography immediate: east-coast urban species (Rainbow Lorikeet, Eastern Rosella, Australian King-Parrot) show high observations in VIC and NSW, with some in QLD, while arid-inland and northern species (Budgerigar, Red-winged Parrot, Red-tailed Black Cockatoo) are more evident in QLD, NT and WA. To explore the details, hover any node for a summary; hover a single bar for the state's count.
